13 Broken Pages (404 Errors)
▼

We found 13 pages returning 404 errors, including key fleet pages (/fleet/, /fleet/buses/, /fleet/sedans/, /fleet/suvs/) and blog posts. These broken links hurt your search rankings and frustrate visitors who land on dead pages.

  • /fleet/ -- Old fleet section (6 broken sub-pages)
  • /our-services/airports/ -- Old service URL structure
  • /our-services/personal/weddings/ -- Old wedding page
  • /services/ -- Generic service page
  • 3 blog posts with broken URLs

Fix: Set up 301 redirects from old URLs to current pages. This can be done in a single afternoon and will recover lost search equity.

36 Pages Missing Meta Descriptions
▼

Out of 87 live HTML pages crawled, 36 are missing meta descriptions. This means Google is auto-generating snippets for these pages, which often results in poor click-through rates from search results.

That's 41% of your pages without optimized search snippets. Each missing meta description is a lost opportunity to persuade a searcher to click your result instead of a competitor's.

Fix: Write unique, compelling meta descriptions (155 characters max) for each page, prioritizing highest-traffic service and location pages first.

Accessibility Score: 61/100
▼

Your website scored 61 out of 100 for accessibility on both desktop and mobile. This score indicates significant issues that may prevent users with disabilities from navigating your site -- and it's also a ranking factor for search engines.

Common accessibility issues include missing alt text on images, poor color contrast, and missing form labels. Given that you offer ADA-accessible vehicles, your website should reflect that same commitment to accessibility.

Fix: Run an accessibility audit and address contrast ratios, missing alt attributes, and ARIA labels. This also reduces legal liability under ADA compliance requirements.

Blog: Only 8 Posts Since June 2025
▼

Your blog launched in mid-2025 and has published approximately one post per month. While this is a start, the content is largely promotional and event-driven rather than optimized for search intent.

Competitors who are outranking you are producing more frequent, longer, and more targeted content addressing specific customer questions (e.g., "How much does a limo to JFK cost?" or "Wedding transportation checklist").

Fix: Increase to 2-4 posts per month. Target informational and commercial keywords. Each post should be 1,500+ words and answer a specific question your customers are asking.

Only 10% of Google Reviews Get a Response
▼

You have an exceptional 4.9-star rating across 1,136 Google reviews -- but only about 10% receive a response from the business. This is a missed opportunity for engagement and SEO signal.

Google has confirmed that responding to reviews is a local ranking factor. Responding to reviews also shows potential customers that you value feedback, encourages more reviews, and gives you a chance to include keywords naturally.

Fix: Implement a review response workflow. Even a brief, personalized thank-you response to every 5-star review will improve local rankings and customer perception.

Instagram Engagement: 0.17% (Zero Comments)
▼

Your Instagram account (@kinglimousine) has 1,282 followers but averages just 2.2 likes per post with literally zero comments across all recent posts. Every post is a static image -- no Reels, no Stories, no video content.

For context, even a small local business should expect 1-3% engagement. At 0.17%, your Instagram is essentially invisible to followers. The algorithm deprioritizes accounts with low engagement, creating a downward spiral.

Fix: Shift to Reels-first strategy with behind-the-scenes fleet content, wedding day moments, and driver spotlight videos. Use Stories for polls and engagement. Consider user-generated content from happy riders.
